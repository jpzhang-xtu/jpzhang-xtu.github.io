Dr. Jianping Zhang (张建平) received his Bachelor’s degree in 2003 from [Xiangtan University](https://en.xtu.edu.cn/) [(中文)](https://www.xtu.edu.cn/) in China. He began his Master’s studies in Computational Mathematics in 2006 and subsequently obtained his Ph.D. degree from the [Dalian University of Technology](https://en.dlut.edu.cn/) [(中文)](https://www.dlut.edu.cn/), Dalian, China, in 2012. He then spent one year as a postdoctoral researcher at City University of Hong Kong. In 2013, he joined the [University of Liverpool](https://www.liverpool.ac.uk/), Liverpool, U.K., as a Research Associate for three years. He is currently a Full Professor in the [School of Mathematics and Computational Science](https://math.xtu.edu.cn/) at [Xiangtan University](https://en.xtu.edu.cn/), Xiangtan, China. 

Prof. Zhang has published numerous research papers in leading international journals such as SIAM and IEEE journals. His research focuses developing imaging analysis techniques for medical image processing and computer vision problems using a range of mathematical tools, such as variational models, PDEs, numerical optimization, and deep learning methods, including
- Variational image modeling for:
  - I.) image restoration and reconstruction, II.) image segmentation (object extraction in medical imaging, bioinformatics, and satellite imagery), III.) image registration, IV.) image fusion, V.) radiomics, and VI.) image inpainting;
- Explainable deep learning and machine learning applied to:
  - I.) medical imaging, II.) remote sensing image restoration, III.) image segmentation, IV.) image registration, and V.) image analysis;
- Unsupervised data clustering, sparse optimization, numerical optimization, numerical PDEs, and fast algorithms.
